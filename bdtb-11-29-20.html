Best Day to Buy the Market

Link to Google Sheets Best Day to Buy the Market

When is the best day to buy the market?....yesterday.

After finding the results of the week, I knew the answer to the month.

For people who dollar cost average or just decide - every month I'm going to invest a certain number of dollars into the market - which day of the week or month continually have the lowest open price? That was the question that crossed my mind. After finding the results of the week, I knew the answer to the month.

The answer? The sooner the better.

Another reinforcer that daily fluctuations do not matter. The idea that the world economy is debt driven, and a method central banks use to assist in paying off large sums of debt is slow, controlled inflation.

For the day of the week, Mondays overall ranked the lowest open price of the week...because the market trends up over time - surpise, surprise! I also excluded entire weeks where there was a holiday.

For the day of the month, I excluded no days - if holiday or weekends were to be removed, might as well skip all of the data. The general trend shows lower open prices at the beginning of the month, only to continually rise until the end of the month were there is a sudden drop, however, I attribute this sudden drop is due to inconsistent month lengths. So again, generally the lowest price is the earliest price because, again...the market trends up over time.

To figure this out I took historic daily VFIAX(link) price data from November 2000 up to November 2020, added identifier columns to the data and then ranked each day of the week within the week, and each day of the month within the month, providing an ordinal rankings for the day of the week/month. Then by summing the total of the ordinal rankings, received the overall score to rank each week/month day.
