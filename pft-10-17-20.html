Personal Finance Tracker

Link to Google Sheets Personal Finance Template

For almost 10 years I have been logging every dollar I have spent. Inspired by my lack of money, income and also books like 'The Millionaire Next Door' I decided to get a hold of my finances and see where my cash was flowing. After years and numerous updates, behold, my current personal finance tracker for public use.

How to :
1. Get a Mint account and connect all your accounts. This will help input transactions and get a less involved/precise overview.

2. The first of every month update the balance sheet section. This is so you have a snapshot to compare accounts and your net worth against other months.

3. At minumum, once a week sit down, log into Mint.com and enter what you have spent into the categories. Better if you jump on for a few minutes every couple of days and enter expenses because if you wait until two weeks, it starts to become a time consuming chore and also, remembering exactly what you were buying could be difficult.

4. Record all line items with a tick mark of cash or credit just to make note, the check mark is available using the Webdings font, lowercase a. However, you should use the tracker (and all expenses) as if they are cash. You are legally liable to pay, so you reflect it in your expenses NOW. When you put money on your credit card, act like it is cash, dont confuse yourself with the delayed timing of a credit card bill. The purpose of using a credit card is not to use money you dont have, but to spend money on things you already would, and ALSO to receive cash back rewards or other rewards for spending.

5. Update category budgets in row 68, ex. cell F68 for discretionary, cell L68 for groceries.

6. Month Overview net worth end of month figures are pulled from the following months balance sheet. So once you fill out the next months balance sheet you can go back and see how your net worth has changed.
